The Dark Night of My Soul

The Tale of a Student Torturing Yourself by Trying Too Hard

Act I: The Ambitious Beginning

The goal was simple, yet deceptively complex: create the perfect COROS 3D watch in Blender. Armed with determination and a real COROS watch on my wrist, I embarked on what would become an epic journey of self-discovery through the lens of 3D modeling.

I started with the watch face—a task that filled me with genuine joy. Using the skills I had learned, I dove in headfirst, scaling, beveling, and expanding with the confidence of someone who had found their rhythm. Each vertex moved with purpose, each edge loop placed with intention. This was it—I was creating something from nothing, and it felt magnificent.

The Dream: My COROS companion and tormentor—the impossible standard

Act II: The Descent into Button Hell

Then came the buttons.

Oh, the buttons.

My own COROS watch sat there on my wrist, mocking me with its intricate details. "Look at these perfectly crafted buttons," it seemed to whisper. "Look at the subtle bevels, the precise indentations, the way each detail catches the light."

My attempts were nothing short of digital torture:

  • Adding objects to delete parts of the main object—a Boolean nightmare
  • Hours spent tweaking, only to realize I'd made it worse
  • The painful realization that sometimes, trying harder isn't the answer
  • Abandoning the project for days, defeated but not broken

I gave up. Not once, but multiple times. Each return to the project brought the same result—no better, no closer to those perfect buttons that taunted me from my wrist.

Act III: The Breakthrough

Then class came, and with it, a revelation that hit like lightning: I was trying too hard.

Sometimes, the path to perfection isn't through suffering—it's through strategic resourcefulness. I discovered BlenderKit, and with it, a beautiful watch model that would become my salvation:

The Model That Saved My Sanity →

With surgical precision, I broke it apart using vertices, extracting the band and those elusive buttons. Finally, I had my high-fidelity watch—a creation that made my overachiever heart sing with joy!

The lesson wasn't about giving up; it was about working smarter, not harder. About knowing when to leverage resources and when to build from scratch.

The Final Result: My hard-won COROS watch render—a testament to perseverance and adaptation

Epilogue: Next Up, The Scene!

With my watch complete—a hybrid of personal effort and strategic resource use—I stand ready for the next challenge: creating the perfect scene to showcase this hard-won creation.

The dark night of my soul has passed, leaving behind not just a 3D model, but a deeper understanding of the creative process itself. Sometimes the greatest achievement isn't in doing everything yourself—it's in knowing when to adapt, when to persist, and when to find a better way.

"In the end, the perfect watch wasn't born from perfection—it was forged in the fires of frustration and reborn through the wisdom of knowing when to change course."
