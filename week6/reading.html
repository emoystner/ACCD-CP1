Programming Design Systems

programmingdesignsystems.com/introduction
"Is google.com a good website because of the look of the search field?"
I love the challenge in this quote - it really brings out the point of the importance of usability and the importance of the value a product has, and that the value is not just found in its design. Is it thinking about the product holistically? Is it user-friendly? Is it helpful? You could have a beautiful search screen design, but if it didn't search as well as Google, then why would people use it?
"Design systems are especially interesting today, because digital products are systems, and designers who code are no longer confined to the creation of design systems that end up in printed manuals. Code allows designers to not just create designs, but build digital systems that create designs."
Love this - design systems are something I am super interested in. Though very intimidating and overwhelming to think about at the beginning. You cannot underestimate their value though!!

"By using a 'man behind the curtain' (or 'Wizard of Oz') technique, the designers were able to present the prototypes directly to children and to directly evaluate their effect."

What Do Prototypes Prototype?

Stanford HCI - What Do Prototypes Prototype?
"If the role is well understood, but the goal of the artifact is to present its functionality in a novel way, then prototyping must focus on how the artifact will look and feel. If the artifact's functionality is to be based on a new technique, questions of how to implement the design may be the focus of prototyping efforts."
This quote effectively breaks down the different focuses prototyping can have depending on what aspects of the design are already established versus what needs to be explored. It provides a clear framework for deciding where to invest prototyping efforts.
"The designer presented the storyboard to her design team to focus discussion on the issues of what functionality the notebook should provide and how it might be controlled through pen and finger interaction"
I loved this example because it feels so simple and familiar. An easy way to show that prototypes can be simple to the point. I am so used to doing hi-fi, this is refreshing!
"It is important to know these rules when working with color, as they enable designers to create graphics that are accessible to a majority of users. However, to master the art of color combination, designers also have to know how color is used in different cultures and contexts, observe current trends in the arts, and develop a personal style based on this knowledge. Because of this, many authors have struggled to create good ways of teaching designers how to think about color combination."
Color theory is so interesting. This quote does a good job of holding the tension of the rules but also all of the other factors (cultures/styles). Definitely something I want to look more into.
